## 5. Princeton WordNet project for semantic recipe data

[WordNet](https://wordnet.princeton.edu/homepage) is a comprehensive lexical database developed by Princeton University that helps organize and classify recipe-related terms and ingredients into meaningful semantic relationships. It groups cooking verbs, ingredient nouns, and descriptive adjectives into sets of synonyms, making it valuable for recipe parsing and understanding.

The database connects cooking terms through semantic relationships, helping recipe scrapers identify similar ingredients and cooking methods. It can recognize that "dice" and "cube" are related cooking actions, or that "bell pepper" and "capsicum" refer to the same ingredient.

**Pricing:** WordNet is free to use and available as an open-source resource for developers and researchers working on recipe analysis projects.

**Key features:**

- A large database of cooking terms and ingredient relationships
- Semantic grouping of recipe-related words and phrases
- Multiple language support for international recipe parsing
- Integration capabilities with popular NLP libraries
- Hierarchical organization of food categories and subcategories
- Advanced synonym recognition for ingredient variations
- Machine-readable format for easy implementation
- Regular updates to the culinary terminology database
